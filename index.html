How image compression works

Every photo is made of pixels, and each pixel stores color data. Compression looks for patterns and redundancy in that data and rewrites it more efficiently. JPG compression is "lossy," meaning it discards some detail the human eye barely notices, which is why you can shrink a photo by 70-80% and still have it look nearly identical at normal viewing size.

This tool draws your image onto an HTML canvas at your chosen dimensions, then re-encodes it as a JPG at the quality level you pick. That whole process happens using your phone or computer's own processor, not a remote server, which is why there's no upload step and no limit on how many images you can process.

JPG vs PNG vs WEBP

JPG is best for photos, where small color shifts are invisible but file size matters. PNG preserves every pixel exactly and supports transparency, which makes it the right choice for logos, screenshots, and graphics with sharp edges or text. WEBP tries to give you the best of both, often producing smaller files than JPG at similar quality, though it's slightly less universally supported by older software.

Picking the right quality setting

  • 90-100%: Near-lossless, for print or archival storage.
  • 70-85%: The sweet spot for websites and social media — small file, no visible difference.
  • 40-60%: Noticeably smaller, some softness in fine detail, fine for thumbnails.
  • Below 40%: Visible artifacts, only for cases where size matters more than appearance.

Common use cases

Students compress scanned assignments to fit college portal upload limits. Online sellers resize product photos so listings load faster on Shopify or Instagram. Developers optimize images before deploying a website, since large images are one of the most common causes of slow page load and poor Core Web Vitals scores. Anyone sending photos over WhatsApp or email can shrink them to avoid hitting attachment size limits.

Turning an image into a PDF

Sometimes a form or application only accepts a PDF, even though what you have is a photo of a document or a scanned page. Once your image is compressed here, the Convert to PDF button wraps it into a single-page PDF sized to match the image's own dimensions, so there's no separate upload step or another tool to open. This is handled by a small library that runs in your browser, so it's just as private as the compression itself.

Privacy and security

Because compression happens on-device, your photos are never transmitted anywhere. There's no server-side copy, no upload log, and no way for this site to see the images you process. This also means the tool keeps working even if you lose your internet connection partway through, and it stays fast regardless of how many people are using it at once.

Frequently asked questions

Is ImageShrink really free with no limits?

Yes. Compression runs in your browser using your device's own processing power, so there's no server cost that would require a paid plan or daily cap.

Do my images get uploaded to a server?

No. The image never leaves your device. All resizing and compression happens locally using your browser's canvas engine.

Which image formats are supported?

You can upload JPG, PNG, or WEBP files, and choose which of those three formats to save the compressed output in.

Can I convert my image to a PDF?

Yes, after compressing there's a "Convert to PDF" button that wraps the compressed image into a single-page PDF, still generated locally in your browser.

Will compressing reduce image quality noticeably?

At 70-85% quality, most photos look nearly identical to the original while being significantly smaller. Lower settings trade more size for visible quality loss.

Can I resize an image without compressing it?

Yes, set quality to 100% and only change the width or height fields to resize without additional compression.

What image size is best for WhatsApp or Instagram?

Under 1MB works well for WhatsApp, and 1080px width is the standard size Instagram uses for feed posts.

Does this work on mobile phones?

Yes, the tool is built mobile-first and works in any modern phone browser without needing an app install, though it can also be installed as one.

Do I need to create an account?

No account or signup is required. Open the page and start compressing immediately.
